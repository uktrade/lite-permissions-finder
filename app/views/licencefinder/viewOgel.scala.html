Please note you will not receive a hardcopy licence from the Export Control Organisation (ECO).

Using SPIRE, you may now export goods and/or software/technology against this OGEL, provided that you:

  • have downloaded and read carefully, a copy of the relevant licence and all the terms and conditions set out within the licence, and have met these in full. See our website for all OGELs (opens in a new tab)
  • include your licence number or OGEL type on supporting documentation accompanying the items you're exporting against this OGEL and in the records you keep

Keep your business information up to date

You must update SPIRE if any of the business information you have registered on SPIRE changes.

This means it's your responsibility on SPIRE to:

  • upload new business information, such as business name, VAT registration number, or address at which records are kept
  • register your OGEL under a new business
  • de-register the OGEL under a previous business

Find out how to update your business information (PDF) (opens in a new tab) .

You must upload your new business information onto SPIRE within 30 days of any changes to your business. Failure to do this will make your licence void.

How to stay compliant

You can find guidance on best practice for effective compliance procedures and advice on strategic export controls in our 'Compliance Code of Practice' document (opens in a new tab) .

Compliance Inspectors may audit your records within 3-6 months of your registration to ensure your business complies with the terms and conditions of the licence you've used. You'll be notified if a visit is needed. Find out more about compliance visits (opens in a new tab) .

Restrictions not operated by the Export Control Organisation

Your application to register for an OGEL was not assessed against other restrictions on exports (for example on antique goods or pharmaceutical products) not administered by ECO. Learn more about these restrictions that are not operated by the Export Control Organisation (opens in a new tab) .

Find out who is subject to financial sanctions in the UK (opens in a new tab) .

Make sure your licence is current

OGELs are subject to change and you must ensure that the licence you're using is current. You can ask to receive email notifications of any amendments to OGELs (opens in a new tab) .

Where to find further information

It is the responsibility of exporters to consult the current export control legislation, familiarise themselves with export controls and make their own evaluation of the need for export licences.

Learn more about strategic export controls (opens in a new tab) .

How to access training

If you're unsure whether your goods, software or technology require a licence, you can receive training from the ECO. We run a number of training courses, seminars and workshops to help exporters understand their obligations under export control legislation. A charge is applied for this service. Find out about dates and details for forthcoming ECO training courses (opens in a new tab) .

Finally, if you require further advice regarding the information in this letter, including how any relevant export control legislation may apply to your particular case, we recommend that you seek independent legal advice.
