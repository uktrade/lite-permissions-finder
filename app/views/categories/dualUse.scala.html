You need a licence to export some items mainly used for civilian purposes but which also have a military use.

They are typically high-tech, advanced products, often with military standard specifications (ie not specifically designed for military use but with performance levels comparable to an equivalent military item). These are called dual-use items.

Many items you might not think need a licence may in fact have a dual use, such as:

  • nuclear facilities, equipment and materials
  • telecommunications and information security
  • non-nuclear materials and chemicals
  • equipment for processing materials into finished goods
  • marine vehicles, vessels and non-navigation equipment
  • navigation systems
  • propulsion systems
  • computers
  • sensors, lasers and real-time data processing
  • other electronic equipment
