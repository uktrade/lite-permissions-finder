Information provided while using this service, including personal information, may be published or disclosed in line with data protection legislation, primarily the Freedom of Information Act 2000 (FOIA) and the UK Data Protection Act (DPA).

The Department for International Trade (DIT) will process personal data supplied in this service in line with the DPA.

The information you provide in License Controlled Items and Services will be processed:

  • on a lawful basis to allow DIT to perform a task in the public interest or to fulfil an official function, and the task or function has a clear basis in law
  • to carry out the processing necessary for a contract DIT has with an individual
  • where the individual has given consent for the processing of their personal data for a specific task

This information will be shared within the department and with selected third parties for purposes connected with DIT performing a task in the public interest or to fulfil an official function and the task or function has a clear basis in law, including:

  • assessment of the goods sold by registrant
  • assessment of licensing applications
  • assessment of the purpose of sale to end-user of the goods

Information will be shared with the following third parties:

  • other government departments
  • Fivium (for SPIRE application support)
  • UKCloud (for hosting SPIRE data)

Aggregated analysis of responses may also be shared with:

  • the Information Commissioner's Office
  • the Government Internal Audit Agency
  • the National Audit Office business partners listed above

Consent will be sought in line with the DPA if there is a need to share information with any other third parties outside of those listed above.

Public authorities are required to comply with a statutory code of practice which deals, amongst other things, with obligations of confidence.

If you want information you have provided to be treated confidentially, in accordance with the FOIA, it would be helpful if you could explain why you would like that information to be treated confidentially.

If we’re asked to disclose your information, we’ll take full account of your explanation, but we cannot give an assurance that confidentiality can be maintained in all circumstances.

Cookies

We store small files known as ‘cookies’ on your computer to collect information about how you browse the site.

Cookies are used to measure how you use the website so it can be updated and improved based on your needs. These cookies aren’t used to identify you personally. You’ll normally see a message on the site before we store a cookie on your computer.

The name of the cookies we use are:

  • PLAY_SESSION - an application session token
  • seen_cookie_message - an indication if cookie message has been displayed
  • FOX_SESSION_ID - an application session token
  • FOX_ORIGIN_ID - an application session token
  • JSESSIONID - a standard Tomcat session token
  • HAPSRVID - a load balancer session token
  • p_dti_session_id - an application session token
  • field_set - an application form management token

What data we collect

You may be asked to log in while using License Controlled Items and Services. If you have previously registered for SPIRE www.spire.trade.gov.uk (opens in a new tab), the username and password will be the same. If you don’t have a SPIRE account you’ll be asked to register for an account.

We collect data including:

  • your name and email address
  • company name, address and telephone number
  • date of birth
  • password and security question answers
  • the selections you make while you use the service

We use this data to:

  • respond to any feedback you send us
  • email you a code to use in the service
  • allow you to register for a SPIRE account
  • gather feedback to improve our services
  • to assess whether we can issue a licence

How long we keep your data

We will only retain your personal data for as long as:

  • it is needed for the purposes set out in this document
  • the law requires us to keep this data for up to 50 years

Where your data is processed and stored

All personal data is stored in the European Economic Area (EEA).

Your rights

You have the right to:

  • ask for information about how your personal data is processed
  • ask for a copy of all the personal data you’ve given to us
  • ask for any mistakes in your personal data to be corrected
  • ask for your personal data to be erased if we no longer need to hold it
  • raise an objection about how your personal data is processed
  • ask that we only process your data in certain circumstances

If your personal data is processed on the basis of consent, you can withdraw your consent at any time.
