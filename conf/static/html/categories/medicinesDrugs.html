Licensed medicines

You can export a medicine if you have a drug manufacturer or wholesaler licence. Read guidance on exporting a human medicine.

Other exports of licensed goods are administered by the Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency (MHRA). Read guidance on how to use the MHRA Portal to submit applications.

Unlicensed medicines – 'specials'

Certain unlicensed human medicinal products can be exported under a specials licence. Specials are products which have been specially manufactured or imported for the treatment of an individual patient after being ordered by a medical professional. Read guidance on the supply of specials.

Controlled drugs

You will need a licence if you are exporting controlled drugs, eg cannabis, morphine. You must register with the Home Office Controlled Drugs Licensing System, and have an individual National Drugs control System export licence for every export. Read guidance on the export of controlled drugs.
