You may need a licence or other permissions depending on where you are sending or taking your items.

Exporting within the EU

You need a European Firearms Pass (EFP) to send the following items to another EU country as part of your personal effects:

  • firearms
  • shotguns
  • related ammunition
  • sights using non-electronic image enhancement

The EFP acts as proof that you can legally possess firearms and ammunition in the EU. To get an EFP you will need to have a valid UK firearm or shotgun certificate. An EFP is provided free of charge.

For temporary exports, you do not need any other type of licence to take or send non-military firearms and related ammunition to another EU country. If you are exporting your items permanently, you will need to find out from your local police authority whether you need an export licence (you will be told this when you apply for the EFP).

Read more about the EFP and how to get one.

Exporting outside the EU

You don’t need an export licence to take non-military firearms and related ammunition to most non-EU countries for personal use.

You must have a valid UK firearm or shotgun certificate, or visitor's firearm or shotgun permit. It must identify your items and must be presented at the place of export.

However, you do need a standard individual export licence (SIEL) to export to some countries.

List of countries for which you need a SIEL
  • Afghanistan
  • Argentina
  • Armenia
  • Azerbaijan
  • Burundi
  • China (including Macau but excluding Hong Kong)
  • Congo (Democratic Republic)
  • Iran
  • Iraq
  • Ivory Coast (Côte d’Ivoire)
  • Lebanon
  • Liberia
  • Myanmar (Burma)
  • North Korea
  • Rwanda
  • Sierra Leone
  • Somalia
  • Sudan
  • Tanzania
  • Uganda
  • Uzbekistan
  • Zimbabwe

Apply for a SIEL through SPIRE.

Antique firearms

You may also need an Arts Council licence if your firearms are over 50 years old, depending on their value.
