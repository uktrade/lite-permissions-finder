You may need a phytosanitary certificate or CITES permit

You must have a phytosanitary certificate to import any plants for planting in the UK. You may also need a phytosanitary certificate for cut flowers and cuttings of plants.

Apply online for a phytosanitary certificate .

You may also need a CITES (Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species) permit if you are importing endangered plants. Find out what species are covered by CITES or apply for a CITES permit.

Not all plants can be imported. Read a summary of which plants are controlled or prohibited.

If you are importing prohibited plant material for scientific purposes, you must get a licence from the Animal and Plant Health Agency. Read about the import of prohibited plant material.
