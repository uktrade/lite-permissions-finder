You may need an import licence if the species is non-native


You normally don't need a licence to import non-endangered live animals. You must however make sure that you follow animal welfare regulations in full at all times.

You do need a licence to import any non-native animal to the UK - apply using form RM01. Read guidance on importing non-native animals.

Non-native animals may also be covered by the Balai Directive. Read about the requirements of the Directive and see a list of what counts as a non-native species.

Some live animals and birds are covered by a general import licence. See a list of general licences for live animals and birds. You can use a general licence without applying for it, but you must follow all the conditions in the licence.

If you need a licence but no current licence exists, you should contact the Animal and Plant Health Agency by email at imports@apha.gsi.gov.uk or by phone on 03000 200 301.

You can apply for an individual licence using form IV58, but you should contact the APHA first. Some animals, such as rodents, lagomorphs and most exotic mammals, need a specific licence. Apply for this licence
