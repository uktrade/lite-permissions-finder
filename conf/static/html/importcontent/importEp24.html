Animal products


You need a licence to import certain products of animal origin. If a general licence for your product already exists, you can use this to import your items. See a list of existing general import licences for animal products. You can use a general licence without applying for it, but you must follow all the conditions in the licence.

You may also need a licence in order to satisfy Common Agricultural Policy (CAP) requirements. Read more about importing food under the CAP.

Fruit and vegetables


You may be able to use the Procedure for Electronic Application for Certificates (PEACH) tool if you are importing fruit or vegetables. PEACH can help you fulfil a number of important requirements, including plant health requirements. Use PEACH, or read more about the service.

Not all fruit and vegetables can be imported. Read a summary of which plants are controlled or prohibited.

You may also need a licence in order to satisfy Common Agricultural Policy (CAP) requirements. Read more about importing food under the CAP.

Organic produce


You must register with an organic control body if you want to produce, prepare, store or market organic produce from outside the EU. You must get a Certificate of Inspection (CoI) for your import from the control body before goods arrive in the UK. You can decide which body to register with based on your needs. See a list of approved organic control bodies.

Read guidance on how to become an importer of organic produce.
