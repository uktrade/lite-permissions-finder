You don't need a licence to export software that is in the public domain, for example software that is freely and legally available to download from a public website.

If you export your item without a licence, you will still be responsible for any penalties incurred if it's later established the export did need a licence.

If you still think you may need a licence, you should contact the Export Control Organisation (ECO) to discuss your export. You can contact them by telephone on 020 7215 4594, or by email at eco.help@trade.gsi.gov.uk.

End-use controls

You cannot export your item without a licence if either of the following is true:

  • you know or suspect that your item will be used in a weapons of mass destruction (WMD) programme or delivery system
  • you are exporting dual-use items that do not usually need an export licence, but you know or suspect that they might be incorporated into military equipment for use in an embargoed country

You must contact the ECO to discuss your export in each of these cases.

Read more about WMD end-use controls and military end-use controls.

Sanctions and embargoes
