Keep your business information up to date

You must update SPIRE if any of the business information you have registered on SPIRE changes.

This means it's your responsibility to use SPIRE to:

  • upload new business information, such as business name, Companies House registration and VAT registration number
  • register your OGEL under a new business or if your site changes
  • de-register the OGEL under a previous business/sites

You must upload your new business information onto SPIRE within 30 days of any changes to your business. Failure to do this will make your licence void.

How to stay compliant

You may find guidance on best practice for effective compliance procedures and advice on strategic export controls in our 'Compliance Code of Practice' document (opens in a new tab) .

Compliance Inspectors may audit your records within 3-6 months of your registration to ensure your business complies with the terms and conditions of the licence you've used. You'll be notified if an inspection is required. Find out more about compliance visits (opens in a new tab) .

Restrictions not operated by the Export Control Organisation

Your application to register for an OGEL was not assessed against other restrictions on exports (for example on antique goods or pharmaceutical products) not administered by ECO. Learn more about these restrictions that are not operated by the Export Control Organisation (opens in a new tab) .

Find out who is subject to financial sanctions in the UK (opens in a new tab) .

Make sure your licence is current

OGELs are subject to change and you must ensure that the licence you're using is current. You can ask to receive email notifications of any amendments to OGELs (opens in a new tab) .

Where to find further information

It is the responsibility of exporters to consult the current export control legislation, familiarise themselves with export controls and make their own evaluation of the need for export licences.

Learn more about strategic export controls (opens in a new tab) .

Although you have used the online tool to register to use the Open General Licence (OGEL) listed above, if you are still unsure whether your goods, software or technology require a licence, you can use the following online resources to double check. These tools will help you to 'self rate' and identify whether your goods are on the Control Lists.

  1. Control List Classification Service. This tool is only available on SPIRE and registration is required. It has been designed to help you search for previous 'Control List Classification/Rating' assessments made by the ECO's Technical Assessment unit. Control List Classification Service
  2. The Goods Checker Database Tool. This tool has been designed to help you undertake key word searches on the specific wording included in the UK Strategic Export Control lists. This tool can help identify definitions as referenced in Control Lists. Goods Checker Database Tool

How to access training

You can receive training from the ECO if you're unsure whether your goods, software or technology require a licence. We run a number of training courses around the country and can also run courses for your business. A charge is applied for this service. Find out about dates and details for forthcoming ECO training courses (opens in a new tab) .
