You do not usually need a licence to export arts and cultural goods that are less than 50 years old.

However, you do need a licence from the Arts Council to export:

  • collections and specimens from zoological, botanical, mineralogical or anatomical collections
  • collections of historical, palaeontological, ethnographic or numismatic interest

If you think you might need a licence you can contact the Arts Council to discuss your export.

Read Arts Council guidance on export licensing for cultural goods.

CITES

You may also need a Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species (CITES) permit if you are exporting items containing animal or plant products, such as works of art incorporating ivory or plumage.
