Cultural goods exports are licensed by the Arts Council. You may need a licence for goods over 50 years old, depending on their value.

You need a licence to export cultural goods on either a temporary or permanent basis.

Read Arts Council guidance on export licensing for cultural goods.

The Arts Council also issue an open general export licence (OGEL) for cultural goods. Read the licence conditions.

CITES

You may also need a Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species (CITES) permit if you are exporting items containing animal or plant products, such as works of art incorporating ivory or plumage.

Read more about CITES.
